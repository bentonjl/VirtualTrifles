The idea of this page is to provide the visitor with background information on each of the characters in the story.

George Henderson

County Attorney

George Henderson has been called to the Wright farm to investigate the murder of John Wright. In the event of a trial, Mr. Henderson will most likely serve as the attorney for the prosecution. He is young and professional in manner, but he often dismisses the female interest in minor details of domesticity....

Henry Peters

Local Sheriff

The middle-aged local sheriff and husband of Mrs. Peters. He is also called out to the Wright farm to examine the scene of the crime. Like Henderson, he gently teases the women about their interest in Mrs. Wright's quilt.

Lewis Hale

Neighboring Farmer

Lewis was the first to discover the crime scene. After entered the Wright farmhouse to ask John about acquiring a telephone, Lewis finds the strangled man and a wife acting very bizarrely. His most notable quote is, "Women are used to worrying about trifles."

Mrs. Peters

Wife of the Local Sheriff

She is a relative newcomer to the town, and never knew Mrs. Wright before John Wright married her. Mrs. Peters is "a slight, wiry woman" with a "thin, nervous face." She is married to the sheriff and prefers to follow the law, often apologizing for the behavior of the men because they are only doing her duty. Mrs. Peters understands loneliness and the world of the female domestic.

Mrs. Hale

Wife of the farmer Lewis Hale

The wife of the farmer Lewis Hale, she is of a heavier build than Mrs. Peters. She resents the condescension the men, and in particular Mr. Henderson, show women simply because of their gender and domestic occupation. She remembers Mrs. Wright as the young Minnie Foster, and she pitties Mrs. Wright. She also feels regret for not having come to visit Mrs. Wright to alleviate her cheerless life.

John Wright

Local farmer, and murder victim

He was commonly considered a good, dutiful man, but he was also a hard man and neglected his wife's happiness. He paid little attention to his wife's opinions and prevented her from singing. The play centers on the motive for his murder.

Mrs. Wright

Main murder suspect

Born Minnie Foster, she used to be a happy, lively girl who sang in the local choir, but after she married John Wright, her life became unhappy and forlorn. Although she does not appear in the play, she is the main suspect in her husband's murder and sends Mrs. Peters and Mrs. Hale to collect a few minor items for her from the farmhouse.
